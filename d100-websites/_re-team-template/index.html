02 — Why luxury teams are invisible to AI search

It isn't about the design. It's about what the machine can read.

AI answer engines don't browse your site the way a prospect does. They parse structured data, topical signals, and authoritative mentions. Most high-end RE sites fail at all three — and the failures are consistent.

i.

No schema markup.

The site has beautiful photography and nothing machine-readable telling Perplexity that this agent sells in this neighborhood at this price point.

ii.

No structured FAQ content.

AI engines preferentially cite sources that answer the exact question being asked. A page of hero images with listings below it does not do that.

iii.

Generic brokerage templates.

A Compass or Sotheby's template routes authority to the brokerage, not to the team. Your name disappears inside a national URL structure.

iv.

No topical authority on neighborhoods.

A single page for "Paradise Valley" with three paragraphs is not authority. Authority is twenty-five pages of deep, structured content on Paradise Valley, Silverleaf, and DC Ranch that cite each other.

The window AEO is where SEO was in 2011. Twelve to eighteen months before every serious luxury team catches on. The teams that structure now will own citations for the next decade.
