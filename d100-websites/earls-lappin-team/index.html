The Team

Four decades of Naples waterfront fluency.

A partnership built on neighborhood-level specificity and the kind of discretion estate-level sellers require.

Bill Earls built his practice on the premise that a handful of Naples zip codes operate as their own market — and that representation in those enclaves requires a fluency most brokers never develop. For more than thirty years, that fluency has translated into record sales in Port Royal and Aqualane Shores, off-market transactions that never touched the MLS, and long client relationships that span multiple Naples properties and generations of ownership.

Aaron Lappin joined the practice after a decade operating at the top of the same geography, bringing contemporary deal architecture and a sharpened feel for the new-construction pipeline reshaping Gordon Drive and the Aqualane tear-down set. Together, the Earls | Lappin Team operates under the John R. Wood | Christie's International Real Estate umbrella and closes volume that places it among the most productive brokerage units in Southwest Florida — $218 million in 2023 and career volume of more than $2.5 billion.

Neighborhoods Represented

Four enclaves. One fluency.

Estate-level work in the four highest-value residential neighborhoods in Naples.

01

Port Royal

Naples' original luxury enclave and still its most expensive address. Deep-water access, club eligibility, and lot economics that rewrite what "waterfront" means.

Typical Range$15M – $50M
02

Aqualane Shores

The quiet-money alternative to Port Royal. Bayfront and canal properties with direct Gulf access, often transacted off-market to a known buyer pool.

Typical Range$8M – $28M
03

Old Naples

Historic walkable blocks between 5th Avenue South and the Gulf. The most location-inelastic neighborhood in Naples — trophy even inland.

Typical Range$5M – $22M
04

Park Shore

Mile-long private beach, boulevard estates, and beachfront towers. A coastline that rewards both new-construction and legacy ownership.
