About the practice

Why a tower specialist matters at this price point.

Most Naples agents will tell a buyer that Bay Colony is a single market. That answer is wrong — and expensive. Bay Colony is six distinct buildings, each with its own floor-plate geometry, HOA reserve posture, assessment history, pet and rental rules, exposure rotation, and resale demand curve. An agent who does not hold tower-level detail will price a Carlysle the way they price a Remington, and a client will leave three hundred thousand dollars on the table or buy the wrong unit.

Leah Ritchey and Amy Becker built Bay Colony Experts at Premier Sotheby's specifically to close that gap. The practice represents buyers and sellers only inside Bay Colony and the immediately adjacent Pelican Bay amenity corridor. The representation is tower-by-tower by design. It is what this team sells, and it is the reason the team's clients consistently transact at better numbers than the market.

The six towers

Tower-by-tower. Pricing, posture, and why it matters.

A frank per-building breakdown from the team's active transaction record. The numbers below are closed-volume medians from the last twelve months of Bay Colony trades.

Remingtoni. The flagship
The top of the Bay Colony pricing curve. Largest floor plates — 4,500 to 7,200 square feet — and the most private elevator access. Assessment history is disciplined and the building's pet posture is strict. The Remington trade is a floor-plan trade, not a square-footage trade.
$7.2M
Median · last 12 mo
Biltmoreii. Grand scale
Largest total unit count among the six towers. Strong amenity program and the best-appointed lobby in Bay Colony. Lower-floor inventory is the sleeper trade in 2026 — the relative value gap versus high-floor Remington has widened.
$5.8M
Median · last 12 mo
Mansion La Palmaiii. Boutique
The smallest tower by unit count. Feels like a low-rise Italian villa, not a high-rise. Scarcity premium is real — inventory routinely trades off-market. A Mansion La Palma buyer typically waits 12 to 18 months for the right floor.
$6.4M
Median · last 12 mo
Triesteiv. Relative value
The best entry-point unit economics in Bay Colony. Tighter floor plates than Remington or Biltmore, but the Gulf exposure and amenity access are structurally identical. The smart trade for a buyer who wants Bay Colony membership without Remington pricing.
$4.1M
Median · last 12 mo
Carlyslev. Quiet entry
The quieter neighbor to Trieste. Lower density, better pet policy, and an HOA culture that actively avoids special assessments. For a pet-owner or a buyer prioritizing cost predictability over prestige, Carlysle is the correct answer.
$3.6M
Median · last 12 mo
Contessavi. Newest build
The newest of the six towers. Modern finishes, tighter envelope construction, and the best energy-performance profile in Bay Colony. Premium per square foot reflects the construction vintage. The logical trade for a buyer who does not want a renovation.
$5.2M
Median · last 12 mo
