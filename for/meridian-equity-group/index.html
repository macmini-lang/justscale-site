01 / The Bottom Line

Meridian has a clean track record in self-storage. $42M in assets, a fully deployed Fund I, and a thesis that makes sense in this rate environment. But from the outside looking in, the capital raising infrastructure is entirely relationship-driven. No digital presence beyond a basic website, no systematic way to reach qualified investors outside your existing network, and no automation between first contact and commitment. This briefing breaks down exactly where the gaps are and what it would take to build a repeatable investor pipeline for Fund II.

02 / Market Context

Where the market is moving.

Self-storage is one of the few asset classes where investor demand is outpacing sponsor supply. Cap rates have compressed from 6.5% to 4.8% nationally since 2022, but rent growth in secondary markets continues at 5-8% annually. That creates a compelling story for investors, but most self-storage sponsors aren't telling it well.

The funds that are scaling fastest in this space aren't doing it through broker-dealer networks alone. They're building direct-to-investor channels that let them control the narrative, qualify leads before a single phone call, and nurture prospects who aren't ready to commit today but will be in 6 months. The sponsors still relying exclusively on personal relationships are watching their raise timelines stretch while competitors with digital infrastructure close faster.

Confirmation + Fund Materials
