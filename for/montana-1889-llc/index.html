01 / The Bottom Line

Montana 1889, LLC operates a specialty coffee house in Helena while simultaneously managing a $10M residential real estate fund under 506(b) exemption. This dual-business model creates unique marketing challenges as the coffee business generates strong local brand recognition but lacks digital infrastructure to support the fund's investor relations needs. With $5M already raised and $5M remaining, the fund requires sophisticated investor communication systems that complement rather than compete with the coffee house's community presence.

Analysis

Montana 1889, LLC currently appears to rely heavily on personal networks and local community engagement for their fundraising efforts. Their website, focused on promoting their coffee house, lacks any direct calls to action or strategic engagement tactics for potential investors. This approach contrasts with more systematic strategies employed by top-performing funds, which digital platforms for targeted outreach and engagement. Without a online presence or strategic outreach efforts, Montana 1889, LLC may struggle to reach a broader audience of potential investors. Competitors in the residential fund management space, such as AEW Global and Harrison Street Asset Management, demonstrate a more structured approach to investor engagement, utilizing digital platforms and strategic outreach to connect with potential LPs.
