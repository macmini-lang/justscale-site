Our Rules

The four rules.

Most builders bid low, change-order high, and disappear after closing. We'd rather lose the bid than break one of these. What follows are the four rules every project we take runs by, and the stake we put behind each one.

01

Five homes a year. That's the cap.

One project manager per build. One architect. One designer. Every project gets the same standard because we never have more than five running at once. We turn down more work than we take on.

If we ever take a sixth, hold us to this page.
02

Every change order in writing. No exceptions.

No verbal handshakes. No "we'll figure it out at the end." If a change isn't priced and signed before work begins, the change doesn't happen. You'll never see a number at closing you didn't authorize on the day.

If a charge appears we didn't write down, you don't pay it.
03

Your architect sits ten feet from your builder.

Architecture and construction in the same building, every day. Decisions that take other firms three weeks happen here in three hours. Fewer change orders. Faster permits. A home that's actually buildable on day one.

If we ever say "we'll have to ask the architect," something has gone wrong.
04

If we miss our date, we eat the carry.

Every project gets a public schedule with weekly milestones. We've hit our dates 96% of the time over the last decade. The 4% of projects that slip get a written reason, and we cover the financing carry until handover.

Late once is on us. Late twice should never happen.

FAQ

Common questions and answers.

The answer is probably below. If it isn't, send a note. We'll give it to you straight.

How long does a custom home take?
From signed design contract to move-in: typically 12 to 18 months for a new build. Three to four months in design, eight to fourteen in construction. Whole-home remodels run six to twelve. We give you a written schedule before construction begins and update it weekly.
Do I need to own land before we start?
Not necessarily. If you're still searching, we'll walk lots with you and tell you honestly which ones will and won't work for what you want to build. A lot evaluation runs about an hour and costs nothing.
How much does a custom home cost?
It depends on the home. A 3,000 sqft custom in our market typically runs between $1.5M and $3M turnkey, including land prep, finishes, and landscaping. We'll give you a real range during discovery, based on your lot, your design wishes, and current material costs. Not a generic per-square-foot number.
Can I bring my own architect?
Yes. We work with outside architects often. If you've already started design with someone you trust, we'll pick up where you are. If you want everything in-house, we have architects on staff. Either way works.
How are change orders handled?
Every change is written, priced, and signed before any work happens. No verbal agreements, no surprise charges at closing. If you change your mind on a finish in month nine, you'll know exactly what it costs in writing, and you can choose to do it or not.
What kind of warranty do you offer?
One-year workmanship coverage on everything we built. Two-year coverage on mechanical, electrical, and plumbing systems. Ten-year structural warranty. And a lifetime promise that if you call us about your home, even fifteen years in, we'll pick up.
Do you build outside your primary service area?
{{SERVICE_AREA}} is home base, but we travel for the right project. Anywhere within a half-day drive is straightforward. Beyond that, we'll have a candid conversation about logistics, costs, and whether we're the right fit.
How do payments and financing work?
Most clients use a construction-to-permanent loan from a local bank. We work with several lenders and can make introductions. Payments to us follow a draw schedule tied to construction milestones. Never up-front lump sums. Final payment is due only after walkthrough and punch list closure.
