01 / The Bottom Line

Antimony Resources Corp. is raising $8,038,065 to capitalize on North America's critical mineral shortage, specifically targeting antimony exploration in a market where China controls 85% of global supply. With the U.S. designating antimony as a critical mineral for defense applications and electric vehicle batteries, the fund positions itself at the intersection of national security and clean energy transition. Their Canadian operations provide strategic geographic diversification away from Chinese supply chains, potentially capturing significant value as antimony prices have surged 228% since 2020 due to supply constraints.

China controls 85% of supply, prices are up 228%, the Pentagon added it to the critical list. This story sells itself. But nobody is hearing it because there are zero ads running, zero content pushing it out, and zero retargeting hitting the LPs who already know you.
Fix: Run 2-3 Meta ad variations built around the supply chain disruption angle. Your existing LPs will see them too, and sometimes people just need to be reminded you are raising.
No speed-to-lead system for inbound interest
When a potential LP fills out a form or responds to an email, how fast does your team follow up? Industry data shows leads contacted within 5 minutes are 21x more likely to convert. Without an automated follow-up sequence, you are losing LP conversations every day to slow response times.
Fix: Set up an automated email and SMS sequence that fires the moment a lead comes in. Compress the gap between interest and first conversation to under 5 minutes.
IR team is not trained to work paid ad leads
Cold traffic from ads is different from warm referrals. Your IR team and SDRs are used to people who already know you. A paid lead has never heard of Antimony Resources. If you treat them the same way, you will burn through qualified investors and wonder why the funnel is not converting.
Fix: Train your IR team on a cold traffic call script. Different opener, different qualification flow, different follow-up cadence. This is the piece most funds skip and it kills their entire paid ads investment.
