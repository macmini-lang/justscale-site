THE BREAKDOWN

What Breaks When the Operator Comes Second

01

Integration Drift

The new management team spends 6-12 months learning the business while value erodes. The operating team was never in the building when the deal closed.

02

Talent Mismatch

A deal person runs an operating company. They know how to buy businesses. They don't know how to run them. The skill sets are not the same.

03

Forced Exits

The fund lifecycle demands a sale whether the business is ready or not. Good companies get sold at bad times because the clock ran out.

04

Misaligned Economics

When the GP earns fees regardless of performance, capital allocation decisions get made differently than when every partner's net worth is on the line.

How Thesis Capital Is Structured Differently

Three decisions Ian Reynolds made before raising a dollar of fund capital.

01

Built the Operator Bench First

Six Executives in Residence recruited, compensated, and ready to deploy into portfolio companies the day a deal closes. Not after. Not eventually. Day one.

02

Long-Term Hold by Design

No predetermined exit timeline. When a business is compounding, Thesis keeps it. The fund structure rewards patience instead of punishing it.

03

Material GP Ownership

Every partner has real capital at stake in Thesis Partners II. Their financial outcome is directly tied to how the portfolio performs, not the management fee.
